    • History, Geography, and Community Insights

    1. Geographical Location:

      • Englewood is a home rule municipality in Arapahoe County, Colorado.
      • Located south of Denver in the South Platte River Valley.
      • Part of the Denver–Aurora–Lakewood Metropolitan Statistical Area and the Front Range Urban Corridor.
      • Altitude: 5,371 feet (1,637 m) above sea level.

    2. History:

      • Founded in 1858 with the discovery of gold by William Green Russell.
      • Established by Thomas Skerritt in the area known as Orchard Place.
      • First road connecting Denver and Orchard Place created by Skerritt.
      • Incorporation in 1903, J.C. Jones became the first mayor.
      • Significant developments, including the Cherrelyn horsecar path and Swedish National Sanitorium.
      • Notable landmarks: Cherrelyn trolley, Swedish Medical Center, and Pirates Cove water park.

    3. Growth and Development:

      • Acquired land in 1948 for McLellan Reservoir, ensuring water independence.
      • Substantial building boom by 1960.
      • Creation of Cinderella City, a large mall, in 1968.
      • Redevelopment of the mall area into a transit-oriented development with a new Civic Center, library, and city hall.
      • Establishment of RTD's southwest light-rail corridor in 2000.

    4. Geography and Climate:

      • Latitude: 39°38′49″N, Longitude: 104°59′31″W.
      • Higher elevation than Denver at 5,371 feet (1,637 m).
      • Similar climate to Denver, classified as cold semiarid (BSk) according to Köppen climate classification.
      • Moderate winds and stable climate due to South Platte River valley location.

    5. Demographics:

      • Population growth from 2,983 in 1910 to 33,659 in 2020.
      • Racial diversity with 87.8% White, 1.5% African American, 1.3% Native American, 1.9% Asian, and 13% Hispanic or Latino (2020).
      • Household distribution and average size: 23.6% with children, average family size of 2.88.

    6. Government and Infrastructure:

      • Federal Correctional Institution, Englewood is named after Englewood but is located in unincorporated Jefferson County.
      • Englewood Civic Center houses the library, Museum of Outdoor Arts, city departments, and courts.

    7. Neighborhoods:

      • Central Englewood divided by Hampden Avenue and Broadway.
      • Quadrants with varying housing stock and characteristics.
      • Notable neighborhoods: Belleview Park, Hampden Hills, hospital district.

    8. Education:

      • Englewood Public Schools serve most of the city.
      • Littleton Public Schools and Sheridan Public Schools serve small parts.
      • Private schools include Saint Louis School, All Souls School, and Humanex Academy.
      • Former Denver Seminary site redeveloped into residential apartments and retail.
